# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Product Thesis

Pixell Video Comparer should not position itself as a generic video player.

It should position as:

`the fastest browser-based visual QA workspace for before/after video comparison`

The sharpest angle is not "watch video side by side."
The sharpest angle is:

- compare outputs fast
- catch quality regressions early
- share evidence with a team immediately
- avoid heavy desktop tooling for common review flows

## Core USP

### Primary USP

Frame-accurate browser comparison for QA teams who need to review visual quality changes without opening heavyweight tools.

### Supporting USPs

- Zero-install workflow for reviewers
- Fast side-by-side and multi-view comparison
- HLS, local file, and image-based inspection in one lightweight tool
- Built-in capture for reporting and discussion
- Static-hosting friendly, so teams can self-host easily

## Who We Are Building For First

### Ideal first users

- Video AI teams improving upscaling, denoise, interpolation, restoration, enhancement
- Streaming/video platform teams checking encoder or playback output quality
- QA and operations teams reviewing production incidents or regressions
- Small post-production or media tooling teams that need quick comparison, not full edit suites

### Not the first focus

- General consumers
- Full editing workflows
- Enterprise DAM buyers with long procurement cycles
- Creative collaboration suites

## Initial Positioning

### Positioning statement

For teams that regularly compare original and processed video outputs, Pixell Video Comparer is a browser-based QA workspace that makes visual review faster, more consistent, and easier to share than ad hoc player setups or heavyweight desktop tools.

### Competitor framing

We should avoid fighting professional NLEs on editing power.
We should avoid fighting observability tools on broad analytics.

We should win on:

- speed to inspect
- ease of access
- no-install review
- focused visual QA workflow

## Distribution Hypotheses

### Channel 1: GitHub-first discovery

Reason:

- technical early adopters already look for lightweight tools on GitHub
- the product is static and easy to try
- dev/tooling communities reward sharp utilities

Execution:

- tighten README and landing page around QA use cases
- publish short demo clips and comparison screenshots
- create release notes around concrete workflows

### Channel 2: Content-led niche SEO

Reason:

- many users search by task, not product category
- long-tail searches are more realistic than broad "video tool" terms

Target query themes:

- compare two videos side by side in browser
- HLS stream comparison tool
- before after video QA tool
- video quality regression review
- compare encoded outputs frame by frame

Execution:

- create feature pages and use-case pages
- create short how-to articles
- use screenshots and example workflows

### Channel 3: Community seeding

Targets:

- GitHub
- relevant Reddit communities
- Hacker News when the product has a clean story
- video AI / MLOps / media engineering communities
- indie hacker and maker channels

Execution:

- show actual problem and actual workflow
- avoid generic launch copy
- publish "here is the exact QA pain this removes"

### Channel 4: Direct outreach to likely teams

Targets:

- startups building video enhancement
- teams working on codec, playback, or streaming QA
- agencies and studios with repetitive review workflows

Execution:

- short outreach with GIF and 2-sentence value proposition
- ask for workflow feedback, not a sale

## Messaging Angles To Test

### Angle A: Speed

"Review output quality in minutes, not in a pile of mismatched players."

### Angle B: QA confidence

"Catch visual regressions before shipping."

### Angle C: Lightweight access

"Open in browser, compare instantly, share evidence fast."

### Angle D: Team review

"Turn visual QA from a solo workflow into a repeatable team process."

## Monetization Hypotheses

### Near term

- free hosted utility to build traffic and workflow adoption
- optional sponsored/private deployment support

### Mid term

- hosted team features
- saved sessions or review workspaces
- annotations and share links
- team history and comparison records
- premium monitoring or reporting features

### Long term

- B2B subscription for media QA teams
- self-hosted commercial package for internal review environments

## What Must Be True To Win

- first-use value must be obvious within 2 minutes
- users must understand the product is for QA, not playback
- screenshots and capture output must help collaboration
- the browser workflow must feel materially easier than current ad hoc methods
- we must identify one segment with repeated weekly usage

## Immediate GTM Priorities

1. Make the homepage and about page communicate one sharp use case.
2. Collect 10 concrete user workflows we want to support.
3. Create 3 messaging variants and test which one gets more interest.
4. Add a lightweight contact or feedback CTA.
5. Capture proof: GIFs, screenshots, before/after examples, sample tasks.
