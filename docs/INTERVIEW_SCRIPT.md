# User Interview Script

## Goal

Understand whether Pixell Video Comparer solves a recurring, expensive, and frequent visual QA problem for a specific segment.

## Target Profiles

Prioritize interviews in this order:

1. Video AI / enhancement teams
2. Streaming / playback QA teams
3. Small media or post-production teams with repeat comparison workflows

## Interview Format

- 20 to 30 minutes
- Focus on current workflow, not feature pitching
- Ask for a real recent example
- Avoid leading questions

## What We Need To Learn

- How often this workflow happens
- How much time and friction it causes
- What tools people currently combine
- What evidence they need to share internally
- Whether fast browser comparison is actually meaningful to them

## Opening

Use this short intro:

"We are trying to understand how teams currently compare original and processed video outputs. This is not a sales call. I want to learn how your workflow works today, where it breaks, and what would actually save time."

## Core Questions

### Section 1: Context

1. What kind of video comparison work do you do most often?
2. What kinds of outputs are you comparing?
3. How often does this happen in a normal week?

### Section 2: Current workflow

4. Walk me through the last time you had to compare two or more outputs.
5. What tools did you open?
6. How did you sync playback or inspect detailed differences?
7. What parts of that workflow felt slow or annoying?

### Section 3: Pain intensity

8. What goes wrong when this review is hard to do well?
9. Have you ever missed an issue because the comparison workflow was weak?
10. Which part is most painful: setup, inspection, sync, capture, or sharing?

### Section 4: Team communication

11. How do you share visual evidence with teammates today?
12. Do you need screenshots, notes, timestamps, or approval records?
13. What makes review discussions inefficient?

### Section 5: Reaction to product thesis

14. If there were a browser-based visual QA workspace for loading, syncing, inspecting, and capturing outputs quickly, where would that fit in your workflow?
15. What would make that immediately useful to you?
16. What would make you ignore it?

### Section 6: Prioritization

17. Which matters more: faster setup, better evidence capture, or deeper comparison controls?
18. Would a hosted browser tool be more useful than a local-only file for your team?
19. If this worked well, who else on your team would use it?

### Section 7: Closing

20. Can we follow up after a test build or prototype?
21. Is there someone else on your team or in your network who has this same problem?

## Optional Demo Prompt

Only after hearing their workflow first:

"I can show a lightweight browser tool for this and get your reaction, but I want your current workflow first so I do not bias the conversation."

## What Counts As Strong Signal

- They do this at least weekly
- They mention setup friction without prompting
- They describe multi-tool workarounds
- They care about sharing evidence, not just viewing
- They say a browser workflow would reduce real friction

## What Counts As Weak Signal

- They compare outputs rarely
- Their current workflow is already smooth enough
- The pain is interesting but not operationally important
- They like the idea but cannot imagine using it repeatedly
